Visualization 1 - Number of Licenses by License Type

For my first visualization, I wanted to highlight the diversity of every Illinois license type. This dataset includes every Illinois professional license, and I wanted to investigate which licenses appear the most in this dataset. I wanted to highlight the most common licenses and see if it is due to higher demands. From this, it seems like many popular licenses are within the medical field, with cosmetology and dental having the second and third highest number of licenses. For my visualization, I put the number of licenses on the x-axis and license types on the y-axis. I chose a bar chart because it is easier to compare categories. For my color choice, I used one color to keep the graph clean and easy to read. In my notebook, I transformed the data by counting how many records appeared for each license type.

Visualization 2: Top Counties Listed by License Status

For my second visualization, I wanted to showcase the different license statuses per Illinois county. The x-axis is the number of licenses, and the y-axis is the counties. For this visualization, I chose a bar chart because I wanted to highlight the relationship between license status and counties. I used one color for the bar chart because I wanted the chart to be easy to read. I also limited the chart to the top 10 counties so the graph would not be too crowded. In my notebook, I removed rows with missing county values, grouped the data by county and license status, and counted the number of records.

For interactivity, I created a license status dropdown menu. This lets users choose which license status they want to view. This makes the visualization clearer because users can focus on one license status at a time instead of looking at all license statuses together.
